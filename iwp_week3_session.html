Root Introduction
Four roots, one idea: the decades of life. Latin number roots tell you exactly how old someone is — if you know them. Read every card before moving on.
🎂 Quick Think: What is the difference between an octogenarian and a nonagenarian?
Both are age words. Both end in -genarian. The difference is in the root — and the root tells you the decade. By the end of this section, you'll know all four age brackets by their Latin origins.
Root 1 — OCT / OCTO · Latin & Greek · eight
oct-
= eight (Latin octo, Greek okto)
octogenarian
noun
A person in their eighties (80–89). From Latin octoginta = eighty.
"Many octogenarians today live active, independent lives well into their late eighties."
octagon
noun
An eight-sided geometric figure. From octo + Greek gonia (angle).
"A stop sign is octagonal — eight sides, instantly recognisable on any road."
octet
noun
A musical group of eight, or a composition for eight performers.
"Schubert's Octet in F major remains one of his most celebrated chamber works."
Root 2 — DEC / DEKA · Latin & Greek · ten
dec-
= ten (Latin decem, Greek deka)
decade
noun
A period of ten years. From Greek deka.
"The decade of the 1960s was defined by social upheaval and nonconformism."
decagon
noun
A ten-sided geometric figure. From deka + gonia (angle).
"A decagon has exactly ten angles and ten sides — double the pentagon."
pentagon
noun
A five-sided figure. Shares the -gon (angle) root with octagon and decagon. From Greek penta (five).
"The US Defense building is called the Pentagon for its five equal sides."
Root 3 — NONA · Latin nonaginta · ninety
nona-
= ninety (Latin nonaginta)
nonagenarian
noun
A person in their nineties (90–99). From Latin nonaginta = ninety.
"The nonagenarian still completed the morning crossword every day without help."
centenarian
noun
A person who has reached 100. From Latin centum (100). Endpoint of the age-bracket series.
"When she turned 100, local papers ran a story about the town's newest centenarian."
Root 4 — SEPTU · Latin septuaginta · seventy
septu-
= seventy (Latin septuaginta)
septuagenarian
noun
A person in their seventies (70–79). From Latin septuaginta = seventy.
"She retired as a septuagenarian and promptly enrolled in a painting class."
