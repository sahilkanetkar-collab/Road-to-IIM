The Hook

You already know the word pedestrian. But did you know that the word expedient — meaning a convenient solution — literally means "freeing the foot from a trap"? Or that an impediment is something that "puts a chain on your foot"?


One Latin root, ped-, unlocks dozens of English words. This week, you'll learn how to read words you've never seen before — by understanding the parts they're made from.

Root 1 — bi / bin

bi– / bin–

Latin · meaning: two
bicycle
noun
A vehicle with two wheels.
"She rode her bicycle to school."
biped
noun
A creature that walks on two feet.
"Humans are bipeds; dogs are quadrupeds."
biannual
adjective
Happening twice a year.
"The biannual report is due in June and December."
biennial
adjective
Happening once every two years.
"The biennial festival draws crowds every other summer."
bilateral
adjective
Involving two sides or parties.
"The two countries signed a bilateral trade agreement."
bifocal
adjective
Having two focal lengths (as in lenses).
"Her bifocal glasses correct both near and far vision."
Root 2 — tri

tri–

Latin/Greek · meaning: three
tricycle
noun
A vehicle with three wheels.
tripod
noun
A three-legged stand.
"The photographer set the camera on a tripod."
trilateral
adjective
Involving three sides or parties.
triennial
adjective
Happening once every three years.
Root 3 — quad / quadr

quad– / quadr–

Latin · meaning: four
quadruped
noun
An animal that walks on four feet.
"A horse is a quadruped; a human is a biped."
quadruple
verb / adjective
To multiply by four; consisting of four parts.
quadrilateral
noun
A four-sided polygon.
"A square is a type of quadrilateral."
Root 4 — cent

cent– / centi–

Latin · meaning: hundred
century
noun
A period of one hundred years.
centennial
noun / adjective
A hundredth anniversary; happening every hundred years.
"The city celebrated its centennial with a parade."
centipede
noun
Literally "hundred-footed" — a many-legged insect.
centenarian
noun
A person who is one hundred years old or more.
"At 102, she was the oldest centenarian in the district."

Fill-in exercises

Complete each word using the correct root

Use what you've just learned. Type the complete word in the blank. The root hint is in brackets.
1. A person who has reached the age of 100 is a c___________. [cent + suffix meaning "person of that age"]
2. An animal with four legs is a q___________. [quad + ped]
3. A lens that corrects two focal lengths is called b___________. [bi + focal]
4. An event that happens twice a year is b___________. [bi + annual]
5. A 100th anniversary celebration is called a c___________. [cent + ennial]
6. A three-legged camera stand is a t___________. [tri + pod]
Key insight: Notice how bi– always means two, tri– always three, quad– always four, and cent– always hundred — regardless of what follows. Once you know a root, you can decode dozens of words you've never seen. Try: bimonthly, triennium, quadrant, centimeter — you already know what they mean.
